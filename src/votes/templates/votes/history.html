Origin of Jeff

Jeff is short for Jeffrey which is a common English name which originated from either:

  • Germanic origin: (Specifically Teutonic origin, a Germanic branch of the Indo-European language family) using a combination of 3 Old German names meaning district, travel, or peaceful plege
  • French origin: A variant form of Geoffrey (which is a Middle French variant of Godfrey or Gottfried) which is the Anglo-Norma form of the Germanic compound of 'god' (gudą) and 'peace' (friþuz).

The meaning of Jeffrey varies slightly per region:

  • American Meaning: God's Peace
  • French Meaning: Divine Peace
  • German Meaning: District, Traveler, or Peaceful Pledge
  • Anglo-Saxon Meaning: Peaceful Gift

Origin of Chen

Chen, meaning "old" is one of the most common East Asian last names with a Chinese origin. It is the 5th most common last name in China and most common last name in:

  • Taiwan
  • Singapore
  • Guangdong
  • Zhejiang
  • Fujian
  • Hong Kong
  • Xiamen

Chen comes from Gui, one of the last names of Gui Man (Duke Hu of Chen), a descendant of Emperor Shun. When Chen was conquered by the Chu state in 479 B.C. the people of Chen took on the last name.

Chen Baxian later created the Chen Dynasty (557-589) which was eventually destroyed by the Sui Dynasty. Fujian was the original home of the Chen clan before it migrated under Trần Kinh to Dai Viet and set up the Trần dynasty which ruled Vietnam.

Chen also is the 150th most popular name in the U.S. with 169,580 people in the 2010 Census using the last name Chen.
